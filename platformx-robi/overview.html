Investing: it’s all about time, not timing

Time – rather than market timing
– is the key.

Most people work for money, but investors make money work for them. Compounding or the ability to generate profit from profits – can significantly boost returns, provided it is put to work over a long period of time.

Automatically reinvesting dividends to buy more shares or units in pooled funds is the easiest way to super-charge your portfolio. Time – rather than market timing – is the key. Investors who start young can earn most from this phenomenon.

Market research...

Its value is demonstrated by new analysis of the world’s biggest markets over 20 years. Research by a well-known financial organisation found that £10,000 invested in the British Market in 1993 over 20 years would have grown to more than £20,700 in terms of share price increases alone. Interestingly, if dividends had been reinvested throughout the period, total returns would have nearly doubled to more than £40,100.

Your money working for you

The threat of automatic reinvestment is that occasionally you can buy more duds, throwing good money after bad. Not all investment strategies work in all places in all times. However, time and a carefully managed portfolio can reap healthy returns. Kuvera is about making your money work for you over time and by keeping your fees low – more of your money is working for you.

Are you new to investing?

If this is your first time you have invested or invested online then relax as we try to make the process as painless as possible.

Firstly, you must decide:

  • what you want to achieve
  • how long you want to invest for
  • the level of risk you are comfortable with.

Spreading risk:

When investing a vital ingredient is balance - getting broad exposure through diversification. This spreads risk – the more risk averse you are the more we diversify for you.

Our Investment Process

At Platform X, our investment process is based on three principles:

1. Spreading risk and opportunity

Rather than investing in a few companies we invest your money in a variety of different investment assets and countries so you don’t have all your eggs in one basket. This is known as diversification.

2. A portfolio tailored to you

Yogi takes account of the reason you are investing, your tolerance of risk, how much money you are investing and over what period of time you wish to invest. This means you get investments which are suited to you and your circumstances.

3. Regularly reviewing your investments

Yogi regularly takes a look at your portfolio to ensure its on track – and will tweak and rebalance things if required.
