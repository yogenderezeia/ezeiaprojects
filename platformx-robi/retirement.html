Retirement Planning Guide

Planning for a richer retirement… whatever age you are…

Ever wondered if you're on track for a comfortable retirement? Whatever your age, there's a plan for you.

We all want to enjoy ourselves when we’ve stopped working so it’s important to do a little bit of planning. Putting something aside for when we get older (and it happens to us all!) is essential.

So, how can you make sure you’ve got more in your pocket for those golden years? Simple: plan effectively.

Too young to care?

If you’re younger focus on managing your debts and put something away you can afford away each month – preferably where you will get a decent return over the long term.

If you’re in your twenties or thirties retirement may seem a long way off - and it's realistic to allow other financial objectives to take priority.

However, even a small contribution is better than nothing and starting an investment pension fund early is a great way to build up a bigger retirement income for later in life. Starting early also means contributions have longer to grow.

Mid-life Challenges

We all face new challenges, with the costs to go with them. There’s a fine balance to be struck between saving for the future and managing debt.

Waste no time in exploring your retirement saving options as soon as you can. Your first port of call should be to find out if your company offers a superannuation pension scheme (super). If so, they will make contributions on your behalf – if so take it as its essentially free money.

Take a long term view on your pension investments. You can afford to take on more risk in the form of shares - as there is a high chance this will pay off over 15, 20 or 25 years' time. Shares generally outperform savings accounts in the long run.

Make sure you budget and debts are under control wherever possible.

It’s never too late

No matter how old you are if you have not started to plan for retirement already, it's not too late to start. It will just require more effort but with an option like Kuvera it’s now much easier. It's imperative that you act now and make the most of your earnings. If you are older, all in all, you should be in a good position to start dedicating some real money towards planning for the future as it’s more likely you have cleared any big debts.

Maximise your contributions

Make the most of pay rises and bonuses to boost your retirement savings, rather than simply increasing your expenditure each time. As you mature it’s a time to take your retirement planning seriously, and that means having a target retirement age and understanding what your lifestyle will look like in retirement. You might not be able to get an accurate picture of your retirement just yet, but you should be thinking about it in broad terms and making sure your financial plans are on track to deliver.

Retirement beckons

As you get closer to retirement start thinking about more control and removing risk from your pension investment plan. This time is perhaps the most important time of all when it comes to retirement planning. Do you have a retirement date in mind? It might not be definitive, but it should serve as a guide. Then calculate the sort of income you want. Perhaps work out a minimum and a nice to have.

Flexibility in retirement

It is becoming increasingly popular to have greater control over income flexibility in retirement, for things like treating yourself to a big vacation, regular trips away, buying a holiday home or helping to support your children and their young families. So it’s wise to think about where you will keep your money, how easy it is to get your hands on it if you need it and how hard it is working for you in retirement.

Pensions are important and making the right decisions about how your pension fund produces cash and income in retirement. And remember, the more you contribute the sooner you can stop working.
